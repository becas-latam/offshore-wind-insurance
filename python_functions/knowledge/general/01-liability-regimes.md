# Liability Regime Analysis

## Knock for Knock (K4K)

- Each party bears its own losses regardless of fault
- Simplifies claims handling — no need to prove negligence
- BUT: K4K only works well when the "knock for knock perimeter" is clearly defined
- Key risk: damage to THIRD-PARTY property is typically NOT covered by K4K — fault-based liability still applies
- If the contract is K4K but the maximum liability cap is low, the Employer may be exposed for large third-party claims

## Negligence-Based

- Liability follows fault — the negligent party bears the loss
- Burden of proof is on the claimant
- More complex claims process, but may provide better protection for the Employer if the contractor is at fault
- Risk: if the Employer is found partially at fault (contributory negligence), they bear a share of the loss

## Maximum Liability Cap

- Always compare the cap to the total contract value and the potential loss scenarios
- A cap of less than 100% of contract value for a major scope (WTG, Foundations) is a risk factor
- Uncapped liability is rare and should be noted as favorable for the Employer
- Check if the cap applies per occurrence or aggregate
