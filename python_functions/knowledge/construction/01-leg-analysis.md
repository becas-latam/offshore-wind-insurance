# LEG Clause Analysis Guidelines

## Key Principles

- LEG 1 (DE1) is the broadest exclusion. If a contractor has LEG 1, the Employer has minimal coverage for defect-related damage. This is a HIGH risk for the Employer.
- LEG 2 (DE2) excludes the defective item itself but covers resulting damage. The sublimit is critical — always compare it to the replacement value of the component.
- LEG 3 (DE3) is the narrowest exclusion and most favorable for the Employer. However, it often comes with a sublimit that must be checked.

## Red Flags

- LEG 1 on any major contractor (WTG, Foundations) = HIGH risk
- LEG 2 sublimit less than 20% of the component's replacement value = inadequate
- LEG 3 without a sublimit on WTG = unusual, verify this is correct
- Different LEG clauses for different WTG components — check if the allocation makes sense (e.g., gearbox should typically have higher coverage than transformer)
- No LEG clause specified = the Employer may have NO coverage for defects — flag as CRITICAL

## Serial Defect Considerations

- WTG components are particularly exposed to serial defects (same defect across multiple turbines)
- If a sublimit is per occurrence but the defect is serial, the sublimit may be exhausted quickly
- Always flag serial defect exposure when WTG scope is involved
