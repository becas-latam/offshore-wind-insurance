# Marine Insurance Conditions Analysis

## Co-Insurance and Waiver of Subrogation

The two key protections for the Employer regarding contractor vessels are:
1. **Employer named as co-insured** on contractor's H&M and P&I policies
2. **Waiver of subrogation** from contractor's H&M and P&I insurers

## Risk Assessment Rules

### If Employer is NOT co-insured AND no waiver of subrogation:
- **CRITICAL RISK**: If the contractor's vessel damages the wind farm (e.g., collision with a turbine foundation during installation), the contractor's insurer pays the claim but can then subrogate against the Employer
- The CAR policy may respond, but the Employer loses its deductible and claims record is affected
- Flag this as HIGH risk with recommendation to negotiate co-insurance or waiver

### If Employer is co-insured but NO waiver:
- Better protection, but the insurer may still argue that co-insurance doesn't extend to all scenarios
- Medium risk — recommend obtaining explicit waiver of subrogation

### If waiver obtained but NOT co-insured:
- Good protection against subrogation, but the Employer cannot claim directly under the contractor's policy if needed
- Low-Medium risk

### Both co-insured AND waiver:
- Best protection — LOW risk

## T&I Contractors
- T&I (Transport & Installation) contractors ALWAYS involve vessels
- If the contractor is T&I or EPCI, marine insurance conditions are CRITICAL
- Supply-only contractors may not involve vessels — marine conditions are less critical but still worth checking for transport phase
